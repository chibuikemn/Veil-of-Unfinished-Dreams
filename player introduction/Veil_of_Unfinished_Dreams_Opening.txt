The world doesn't end. It softens.
You fall through darkness that tastes like copper and forgetting. There is no ground to 
catch you—only the slow erosion of everything you thought was solid. Your body, 
wherever it lies now, has become something distant. A shell breathing on its own.
Here, in this place between waking and loss, you land gently.
The air hums with half-remembered music. The light comes from nowhere and 
everywhere—pale, like dawn seen through closed eyelids. You stand in what might be a 
room, or a field, or the edge of an ocean that never moves. The walls breathe. The floor 
remembers footsteps that were never taken.
And you are not alone.
Another figure stands across from you, equally lost, equally real. Their edges blur 
slightly, as though the dream hasn't decided what shape they should hold. You 
recognize nothing about them—and yet, somehow, you know they are here for the 
same reason you are.
You were not meant to be here together. This realm was not built for two.
The world shifts. A low vibration runs through everything, like the moment before 
thunder. You feel something pulling at your chest—urgent, aching, unfinished. It 
whispers a name you haven't spoken in years, or maybe never spoke at all. It demands 
to be seen.
Your dream.
The one you left behind. The one you never let go of.
And when you glance at the other soul beside you, you see it in their eyes too—another 
dream, equally hungry, pressing against the fabric of this place. The Veil trembles. It 
cannot hold both.
Something in the distance begins to take shape. Not fully formed—just the suggestion 
of a door, or a road, or a mirror. The realm waits for you to move. It waits for you to 
choose.
But here is the truth the Veil does not say aloud:
Only one dream can survive intact.
The ground beneath you pulses once, twice. A quiet warning. The other person looks at 
you—not with hostility, but with recognition. You are both trapped here. You are both 
carrying something too heavy to put down.
The Veil does not care about fairness. It cares about completion.
And it is waiting to see which of you will sacrifice first.
What do you do?
